IT-Autobiography

Dit is mijn IT-Autobiography. Hier schrijf ik, in het Engels, hoe IT in mijn leven is gekomen en hoe ik er nu mee bezig ben.

My name is Michiel Segers, I’m a 17 years old IT student at Immaculata institute. This I going to be my IT-autobiography. How IT has changed my life and what I’m going to do with IT in the future.

My first contact with IT was trough gaming. My dad bought a laptop so he could manage some documents easier. I was 5 years old and my brother 7, it was quite the experience for us. We bought a game about dinosaurs and played it when we could. I was always a gamer, from the beginning of life. We had a Nintendo Gameboy and a PlayStation. Later on my dad bought a desktop so everyone could work on it. I didn’t do anything spectacular on that PC I just played games. My dad would always say: ‘Michiel when you’re going to high school you should do something with PC’s so I never have to pay for the company who clean my computer.’ I would always reply that I wouldn’t do it because it was boring. But now here I am in my second year IT.

I’m not the kind of person who is going to program a program at home or just look at the new Ethernet cables. I’m still a gamer. I mostly just game on the evening or just listen to music. I spend a whole lot of hours at my PC. I spend around 4/5 hours on a typical school day. This is just in the evening when I get home from school. When I’m working for school i’ll be with my PC. Either working on it or listening to music while I’m studying something. Many people say it’s bad and you can’t concentrate but it makes the work more bearable because I really hate school. I’m really fascinated by how games are made. For me it’s a mystery how they get them to work. I’m not really interested in the code and I’m not going to learn it. I’m really just fascinated. I don’t see myself as an expert. I know a little more than the average person but I’m far from expert.

I’m not really a guy who looks to the future and asks what will come and I really don’t know what I’m going to do in the future. I’m not interested in IT at all but it’s the most interested thing there is to study. Hopefully I eventually find something I like but I really doubt it. But I really want a college degree because money is very important. I’m just a bit scared for college because my programming is quite bad and I’m going to need it in college. But I’m probably going to be fine.
